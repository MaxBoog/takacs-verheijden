Barbara Takacs

Barbara studied Social Psychology at the University of Amsterdam. She then pursued with enthusiasm a career as a behavioral trainer. In the first years she worked for various Training Agencies. She trained police officers, female civil servants, upcoming talent, and managers from all kinds of sectors. In recent years she focused almost exclusively on universities because it provides a context that is both challenging and comfortable to her. She coaches individual scientists and their teams to take the next step.

With Ben, she designs and facilitates tailor-made programs in Academic Leadership. Her style is facilitating, but also confronting if necessary. Barbara lives in Haarlem with her husband. She is a mother of three children. They like to travel and enjoy local nature. Yoga and running help her to relax.

Ben Verheijden

Ben studied Literature in Amsterdam in the seventies. He then became a teacher, a school counselor, an assistant-principal and in the meantime also a father of four. In the nineties a sudden whirlwind took him to Transactional Analysis and NLP, to Reiki, Inner Child Work, Council and Vision Quest - and he facilitated workshops in personal growth in various countries.

As of the turn of the century he focused on training and consultancy work in higher education.

Quasiba Zeggen

Quasiba studied political Science (MA) and later also psychology (BA) at the University of Amsterdam. She worked for twenty years in higher education as a teacher and management trainer. She then chose to continue as a self-employed professional. She trains leadership skills for various clients including Takacs&Verheijden. Her focus is on communication. In her view it always boils down to: how do you frame and phrase your message? This can be related to feedback and bad news, but also on coaching and collaboration.

Her activities are tailor-made and coincide with the client's vision and mission. She works with humor, compassion, and clarity. Both analytical and empathic. Leadership development is the development of an organization and its employees. When people are willing to listen and understand each other the work becomes both efficient and fun.

Erika Pape

Erika studied Educational Science at the University of Groningen and started her career as a trainer at the Teacher Education Program. Soon after that she switched to the corporate world for the next 25 years and held jobs such as Marketing Communication Strategist and later as an HR professional.

Since a few years, Erika works as a self-employed coach, with more than 20 years of experience in this field under her belt. Her heart lies in talent development, leadership development and collaboration. Erika specializes in coaching professors, lecturers, and executives. She is also trained as a team coach and brings teams to perform on a higher level. She loves traveling, pushing her boundaries, Haarlem, the dunes, the sea and her three young adult children.

Koosje van Frankenhuysen

Koosje studied Organizational Psychology at the University of Amsterdam. She then started in 1996 as a self-employed trainer, coach, and consultant. The first 20 years she developed her talents in as many fields as possible. She honed her skills by working for various agencies and an enormous diversity of organizations. Today she focuses on the three themes: Leadership, Vitality and Team Development. These three contain everything she has to offer.

The challenge for Koosje is to tailor a unique program with for every group or team. She brings both the participant to a higher plan and facilitates the development of the organization. Offering openness, a clear structure, and enhancing personal responsibility in an atmosphere of safety and trust, she reaches tangible and positive results. With a taste for a Burgundian lifestyle and love for food, art and culture she enjoys life in the heart of Amsterdam, with both her teenagers.

Esther Schmal

After a short flirtation with Law School, Esther enjoyed studying Political Science (International Relations) in Amsterdam and Berlin. Then, to do something quite different, she pursued a career in Training and Advice, focusing on developing people and organizations. A series of challenging HR jobs then followed, among others for TU/e, ASML and Avans.

Several years she combined Mid-Career & Executive Coaching with Organizational Advice and facilitating development processes. Her mixture of ‘head, heart and humor’ works well with high-level knowledge institutes and high-tech organizations. The cooperation with Takacs & Verheijden is caused by a personal click, a shared perspective on strategy and leadership development, and a systemic approach to complex issues. Esther lives with her partner in Brabant, likes spinning and long walks. She supports a sustainable way of life and visits flea markets, cultivates a vegetable garden and foraging in nature.

Liesbeth Halbertsma

Liesbeth started her career at Boer & Croon Management Consultants after studying Andragogy in Amsterdam. After that she was statutory director of Nieuw Elan and of the Baak Management Centrum VNO-NCW.

She now enjoys working for universities, teams or departments who wish to articulate their goals and strategy as a team. Through designing and facilitating one or more working conferences, important steps are often taken. If cooperation and collaboration is suboptimal a focused meeting with the team can be helpful. Liesbeth facilitates clients to develop the future of their organization. She is deeply involved in the universities of Leiden and Amsterdam. She finds her work very inspiring. In recent years, she has also consciously focused on working with young people and on her role as supervisor.

Studying and doing research take an important place and regularly lead to publications. And she enjoys life, together with her husband, in their garden and peddling their canoe.

Jan Pieter Weening

Jan Pieter studied Labor and Organizational Psychology and Social Psychology at the University of Groningen. After his studies he focused on the development of people and organizations. After three years of being employed by the University of Groningen, he chose self-employment in 2009. He still likes to work as a trainer/coach in Academia in developing soft skills, team development and organizational improvement.

Jan Pieter believes that cooperation and collaboration, allow for better results and more fun. By connecting confrontation, relativizing, humor and authenticity, his trainings are experienced as meaningful and lively. He works on a positive basis and is convinced that having fun does not mean you’re avoiding the hard work. He lives with his wife and three children, loves to travel and on his own, and doing sport is for him both effort as well as reflection and relaxation, ‘mens sana in corpore sano’.

Jasmijn Mioch
  • Presenter, interviewer, and conference chairperson since 2003
  • Strategic Adviser to board members since 2011
  • Expert in blended learning and implementation strategist since 2017
  • Designer and facilitator of numerous interactive online sessions since 2020

Koen Mertens

Koen completed a coaching-training ‘avant la lettre’ in 1989 and has since worked as a trainer, coach and facilitator in leadership programs. He has the privilege of working together for more than thirty years with a wide range of inspiring (inter)national colleagues in various development assignments. His expertise lies in facilitating personal development in the behavioral perspective of leadership.

Koen likes to work with value-driven people who excel in their field of expertise and want to develop their leadership skills. He creates a setting in which people can explore and learn through a combination of safety, challenge, fun and reflection. Koen ensures that participants start managing their development themselves.

He recharges himself by making hikes with friends, preferably in the mountains. As a bassist, he is the leader in a cover band where his wife is the lead singer. They are parents of two teenagers at home in Breda and a son who works and studies in Rotterdam.

Michelle Geerlings

Michelle studied Industrial Design (BSc.) at the University of Twente and did her master Strategic Product Design (MSc.) at TU Delft. During her study she was active at a Yes!Delft startup and founded her own company ‘Studio Mies’. She has gained cultural knowledge and experience by living in various countries and cultures. She studied in Sweden, helped companies in Japan with their vision and strategy and supported a startup in Kenya. Michelle is now working as a visual strategist at Flatland Agency. She facilitates teams to develop their strategy and vision by making the process visual.

She focuses on articulating the customers’ questions and then developing and guiding an appropriate visual journey. Michelle is enthusiastic about themes focused Sustainable Development Goals and the Design Thinking method. Both physically and online, Michelle quickly draws a visual representation of the conversation.

Working with Michelle gives energy, promotes creativity and enthusiasm. She is flexible, fast thinking and loves to help with solutions to complex issues. Her leadership style is characterized by finding and supporting the strength in others, by delivering custom-made solutions and providing tools.

Hans Bergstra

After his studies in Business Administration, Hans worked for 10 years in various positions in business. What drives and what stops individuals in their development intrigued him the most. That was why he chose to become a coach for individuals, teams, and organizations. Since 2000 he does this from the experience that rest, relationship and results go together. The lack of results can often be traced back to relationship problems, and this often comes from the lack of inner peace. Haste makes waste, rest works miracles.

From this point of view, he combines his competences as a mindfulness trainer, a systemic supervisor, and a professional advisor. He feels at home in universities and beyond, from work floor to board room. It is always a question of letting things flow where it is stagnating, so that all those involved can work together in a way that is meaningful to them.
